Where the Data Comes From

Four data layers. Research runs first, then CRM and manual overrides fill gaps. The principle: never penalize a company for a data gap we created.

🔍
Brave Web Search
Layer 1 — Primary Research

4 targeted searches per company: (1) general company info — employees, HQ, valuation, funding. (2) IT job listings — "Jamf admin" or "Mosyle engineer" signals confirm the Mac MDM tool. (3) Glassdoor/Reddit reviews — employees mentioning Mac vs Windows laptops. (4) Stackshare/tech stack — Okta, Google Workspace, identity tools.

🤖
Claude AI (Haiku)
Layer 1 — Extraction

After each Brave search, Claude Haiku reads all results and extracts structured fields: employee count, HQ city, founding year, valuation, funding round, hardware fleet, MDM tool, productivity suite, work model, growth rates, and key investors. Replaces hours of manual research per company.

🏢
Salesforce CRM
Layer 2 — CRM Backfill

Revivn's Salesforce export provides ZoomInfo employee counts, US employee percentages, billing city/state for HQ, account owners, and opportunity stages for every account ever touched. Only fills nulls — research data always takes priority. Also carries CRM stage and owner for any known account.

✏️
Manual Overrides
Layers 3 & 4 — Ground Truth

High-confidence corrections for 25+ well-known companies where web research returns stale data. Headcount overrides (e.g., OpenAI 3,000 · Anthropic 3,800 · Anduril 3,500) always win when our verified number exceeds research. Work model overrides correct inferred policies for companies with publicly confirmed stances.

The 9 ICP Fit Dimensions

Each dimension scores one aspect of structural fit. Weights were set based on Revivn's actual top 25 ACV accounts and the ITAD business model — device pickup volume, Mac likelihood, and operational reachability.

👥16 pts
Employee Count

Sweet spot: 1,000–8,000. Fast-growing sub-2,000 companies get a 2.5–4× forward-looking multiplier. International HQ companies are scored on US-adjusted headcount — only US employees represent actual pickup volume for Revivn.

🏭16 pts
Industry Vertical

Mac adoption varies from ~95% (AI/ML) to ~5% (Construction). 40+ industry categories mapped to 4 tiers based on Revivn's account history. Tier 1 (AI/ML, SaaS) = 16 pts. Disqualified sectors (Retail, Oil & Gas) = 2 pts — not zero, since exceptions exist.

🔧14 pts
Tech Stack

The strongest confirmed signal. Jamf, Mosyle, and Kandji are Mac-only MDM tools — if a company uses them, we know they have Macs (+7). Okta correlates 90%+ with Mac orgs (+4). Google Workspace is an 85-90% Mac indicator (+3). Microsoft 365 = Windows signal (0).

📍8 pts
HQ Location

Revivn's drivers operate primarily out of SF and NYC. SF/NYC = 8/8. Secondary hubs (Seattle, Austin, Boston) = 5/8. Other US = 3/8. Near-international (Toronto, London) = 1/8. International = 0/8. Revivn's ITAD operations are US-only.

💰8 pts
Financial Maturity

Structured ITAD programs require capital and procurement infrastructure. Public companies and unicorns ($10B+) = 8/8. Public and private late-stage score identically — a $10B private unicorn is no less financially mature than a public company of the same size.

🌎8 pts
NA Concentration

Absolute US employee count, not percentage. Adobe at 30% US sounds bad — that's still 13,000+ US employees. We compute global × US% to get the real pickup volume. 450+ US employees = 8/8. Threshold-based scoring down to <50 US employees = 1/8.

🏢8 pts + cap
Office Presence

The hardest constraint in the model. Revivn sends drivers to bulk-pick up hardware from offices. Fully remote = devices at 500 employee homes — individual pickups destroy unit economics. Fully remote companies are capped at 45/85 ICP Fit regardless of all other scores. Remote-first = capped at 60/85.

🖥️6 pts
Hardware Fleet

Confirmed fleet data from Jamf job postings, IT admin discussions, and review sites. Intentionally kept low at 6 pts — only 30% of companies have public hardware data. Weighting it higher would penalize the 70% we simply don't have data on. Mac likelihood flows through Tech Stack and Industry instead.

📅3 pts
Company Age

Post-2000 companies built IT infrastructure in the Mac era — Jamf, Okta, cloud-native from day one. Pre-1985 companies carry Windows-legacy risk from decades of Dell/Lenovo procurement relationships. A tiebreaker dimension, not a disqualifier.

⚡

Breakout ε — The Forward-Looking Signal

Standard ICP scoring systematically underrates companies that look "too small" today but are on a trajectory to be massive accounts within 24 months. Breakout ε fixes this. OpenAI in 2021 would have scored ~40 ICP Fit (only 500 employees) — but their Breakout would be 19/19. The ε term captures what valuation already knows.

10 pts
Valuation Anomaly

A $5B company with 500 employees means VCs are pricing in 10× current scale. Scored on valuation tiers: $10B+ = 10 pts, $3B+ = 8, $1B+ = 6. Also computed from the current_valuation field as a fallback — data gaps don't suppress high-valuation companies.

7 pts
Investor Quality

Tier-1 backers (a16z, Sequoia, Benchmark, Microsoft, Google) are the most reliable leading indicator of a company reaching the 1,000–5,000 employee sweet spot. Every single one of Revivn's top 25 ACV accounts has tier-1 investors. This is the strongest correlation we found.

2 pts
Category Creation

Companies defining new markets (Anthropic in foundation models, Wiz in cloud security) have compounding growth moats. They're not competing for market share — they're creating it. Small signal, but meaningful for breaking ties at the top.

Known Limitations

The model is honest about what it doesn't know. These are the areas where manual enrichment would most improve precision.

⚠️
Hardware data: only 30% confirmed

70% of companies use the inference engine (industry + MDM proxy signals). Confirmed Jamf/Mosyle data would move many inferred scores to confirmed.

⚠️
Revenue/growth data: ~90% null

The growth multiplier only fires when YoY data was found. Many hyper-growth companies may be under-ranked because their growth rate wasn't in public search results.

⚠️
Score compression at 82–88

Several companies cluster in this range. Tiebreak on: (a) data quality — fewer inferred fields = more reliable, (b) recency of funding, (c) current CRM stage.

⚠️
International companies with US presence

Companies like Pennylane (Paris HQ, 144 US employees) are correctly down-ranked by the US-adjusted headcount model but still appear due to high Breakout ε. Treat as speculative targets.
